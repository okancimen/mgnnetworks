2. What information we collect

When you submit an application or use our website we collect:

  • Student name, age, school, and country
  • Student and parent email addresses
  • Application responses (motivation, department preference)
  • Eduentry psychometric assessment results
  • Payment information (processed by Stripe — we do not store card details)
  • Work deliverables submitted during the programme
  • Basic website usage data (page views, referral source)

3. How we use your information

  • To review and process Fellowship applications
  • To communicate programme information to students and parents
  • To assign students to departments based on assessment results
  • To process payments via Stripe
  • To generate reference letters and portfolio documents
  • To improve our programme and website

We do not use your information for advertising, profiling, or automated decision-making beyond the assessment-based department matching.

4. Who we share information with

We share minimal data with the following third-party services, solely to operate the programme:

  • Stripe — payment processing (Singapore/US)
  • Eduentry — psychometric assessment platform
  • Google Workspace — email, Drive, document storage
  • Slack — student and mentor communication
  • Atlassian (Jira) — project and task management
  • Notion — programme hub and knowledge base

We never sell, rent, or share personal data with third parties for marketing purposes.

5. Data retention

We retain student records and deliverables for a minimum of 3 years after programme completion, to support reference letter verification and alumni enquiries. You may request deletion at any time (subject to legal obligations).

Terms of Service

8. Programme enrolment

Enrolment in the Global Career Fellowship is confirmed upon receipt of payment. By paying, the student (and their parent or guardian if under 18) agrees to:

  • Complete assigned briefs to the standard required for approval
  • Communicate professionally via Slack and Jira
  • Not share programme materials, briefs, or company information with third parties
  • Represent work as their own — any plagiarism or AI-generated submission passed off as original work results in immediate termination without refund

9. Reference letters

A reference letter is issued only upon satisfactory completion of the programme. "Satisfactory completion" means all briefs submitted during the enrolled term received a score of 7/10 or above, or were revised to that standard. The letter is signed by the Managing Director and reflects only work actually completed and reviewed. Magenta Networks does not issue letters for incomplete or unapproved work under any circumstances.

10. Refund policy

  • Cancellation before onboarding begins: full refund within 7 days of payment
  • Cancellation after onboarding begins: pro-rated refund for unused completed months
  • Completed months: non-refundable
  • Programme cancellation by Magenta Networks: full refund issued within 14 days
